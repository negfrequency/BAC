The Baltimore Algorithms Club is a club devoted to collaborative philosophical problem solving.

Relatively new to Baltimore, we seek to identify unique problems, and regardless of experience, work together in sharing knowledge and building software to tackle these problems, while enjoying a few brews on the way.

The 2017-2018 season will focus on the NHL's Stanley Cup by building scrapers, a database, and a model to predict the outcome of games as well as the betting spreads associated with them. Due to the small learning curve, code is usually written in python. We host meetings at Snake Hill in Baltimore every other Wednesday at 8PM to 10PM sharp starting September 6th, 2017. We encourage bringing sketch paper as opposed to a computer.

If you would like to join feel free to show up at our next bi-weekly Wednesday meeting or contact us at baltimore.algorithms@gmail.com. You can also hop on our Slack channel.
